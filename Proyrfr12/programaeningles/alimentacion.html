Proper Pet Nutrition

Since time immemorial, dogs have been faithful companions and an important part of any family. In addition to providing them with love, commitment, and responsibility on our part, they need care to stay healthy (regular check-ups with the veterinarian) and proper nutrition.

Feeding your dog may seem like a simple decision: just stand in the pet food aisle at the supermarket or pet store and choose from the variety of available foods. However, what's good for one may not necessarily be good for another.

There are foods that contain an adequate amount of proteins, carbohydrates, fats, and other nutrients adjusted not only to your pet's breed but also to its age, physiological state, and even its daily activity level, so that your dog receives nutrition tailored to its needs.

The Positive Impact of Pets

On a scale of 1 to 10, how much do you like pets? According to a survey conducted by the Public Opinion Center of the Universidad del Valle de México with 730 people in November 2020, the average rating was 8.7. The experience of having a pet at home has been good or very good for 95% of respondents, mainly due to the companionship and affection they provide.

But that's not all: respondents believed that pets improve, to a greater or lesser extent, the quality of life of older adults, people with disabilities, those without children, and/or those who live alone.

It has been said that daily coexistence between people and their pets creates bonds that positively impact the development of activities and mental well-being. Just observe them to verify how affectionate, loyal, and life companions they can be, qualities that have become even more valuable due to the recent pandemic.

In Mexico, 69.8% of households had a pet, according to data from the 2021 National Self-Reported Well-Being Survey (ENBIARE) by the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI). Of the nearly 80 million pets, 43.7 million were dogs, 16.2 million cats, and 19.9 million other species (see graph).
