Revenue Streams

- Sterilization fees paid by pet owners.
- Donations and sponsorships from organizations and companies.
- Membership programs.
- Grants and government funding.
- Fundraising events and crowdfunding campaigns.





Value Proposition

- Sterilization fees paid by pet owners.
- Reduction in the number of homeless animals.
- Improvement in pet health and longevity.
- Decreased risk of certain types of cancer and reproductive diseases.
- Less problematic behaviors related to heat cycles.

Customer Relationships

- Education and awareness programs.
- Accessible and affordable sterilization services.
- Post-operative support and veterinary consultations.

Channels

- Social media and traditional media campaigns.
- Informational brochures and posters at veterinary clinics and shelters.
- Collaborations with animal welfare organizations.
- Community events and health fairs.

Customer Segments

- Pet owners.
- Shelters and animal rescue organizations.
- Veterinarians and veterinary clinics.
- Governments and municipalities.

Key Resources

- Surgical equipment and materials.
- Veterinarians and trained staff.
- Adequate facilities for performing sterilizations.
- Funds and grants for free sterilization programs.

Key Activities

- Performing sterilization surgeries.
- Education and training on pet care and sterilization.
- Organizing sterilization campaigns and events.
- Post-operative monitoring and support.

Key Partners

- Veterinary clinics and hospitals.
- Non-governmental organizations (NGOs) for animal protection.
- Local and national governments.
- Companies and sponsors supporting the cause.




Cost Structure

- Costs of equipment and medical supplies.
- Salaries and fees for veterinarians and staff.
- Costs of advertising and educational campaigns.
- Operational expenses of facilities and sterilization programs.
